About Kenya Reports Archive

Preserving Kenya's unimplemented reform documents for public access and accountability

Our Mission

The Kenya Reports Archive was created to address a critical gap in our national discourse - the systematic documentation and preservation of official reports that contain solutions to Kenya's most pressing challenges but were never fully implemented.

Since independence, Kenya has invested billions of shillings in commissions, task forces, and special investigations that produced detailed recommendations. While these reports often make headlines when released, they frequently disappear from public consciousness without meaningful implementation.

The Problem We're Addressing

Our research has identified several key issues:

1. Report Disappearance

Many important reports become inaccessible shortly after launch, with original copies disappearing from government websites and physical copies available only in select libraries.

2. Implementation Deficit

Analysis shows less than 30% of recommendations from major commissions have been fully implemented, with many completely ignored.

3. Public Awareness Gap

Most citizens are unaware of the specific recommendations contained in these reports, making accountability difficult.

4. Historical Amnesia

Similar issues keep recurring because lessons from past investigations aren't institutionalized.

Our Methodology

We employ a rigorous process to ensure the archive remains accurate and useful:

  1. Report Identification

    We identify all major government-commissioned reports through parliamentary records, media archives, and expert consultations.

  2. Verification

    Each report is verified against original sources to ensure we have complete, unaltered versions.

  3. Analysis

    Our team analyzes each report to extract key findings, recommendations, and implementation status.

  4. Standardization

    Reports are presented in a consistent format for easy comparison and reference.

  5. Public Engagement

    We create accessible summaries and discussion guides to facilitate public understanding.

Why This Matters

This archive serves multiple critical purposes:

Accountability

Provides citizens with tools to hold leaders accountable for implementing recommendations.

Institutional Memory

Preserves institutional knowledge that might otherwise be lost.

Research Resource

Serves as a valuable resource for researchers, journalists, and policymakers.

Democratic Participation

Empowers citizens to participate meaningfully in governance discussions.

Our Team

The Kenya Reports Archive is maintained by a dedicated group of researchers, archivists, and digital rights activists committed to open governance. While we maintain editorial independence, we collaborate with:

  • Academic institutions
  • Civil society organizations
  • Media partners
  • Concerned citizens
