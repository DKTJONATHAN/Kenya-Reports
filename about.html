About Kenya Reports Archive

Preserving Kenya's unimplemented reform documents for public access and accountability

Our Mission

The Kenya Reports Archive was created to address a critical gap in our national discourse - the systematic documentation and preservation of official reports that contain solutions to Kenya's most pressing challenges but were never fully implemented.

Since independence, Kenya has invested billions of shillings in commissions, task forces, and special investigations that produced detailed recommendations. While these reports often make headlines when released, they frequently disappear from public consciousness without meaningful implementation.

The Problem We're Addressing

Report Disappearance

Many important reports become inaccessible shortly after launch, with original copies disappearing from government websites and physical copies available only in select libraries.

Implementation Deficit

Analysis shows less than 30% of recommendations from major commissions have been fully implemented, with many completely ignored despite their potential impact.

Public Awareness Gap

Most citizens are unaware of the specific recommendations contained in these reports, making accountability difficult and perpetuating cycles of unaddressed issues.

Historical Amnesia

Similar issues keep recurring because lessons from past investigations aren't institutionalized, leading to wasted resources on new commissions for old problems.

Our Methodology

1

Report Identification

We identify all major government-commissioned reports through parliamentary records, media archives, and expert consultations. Each report is evaluated for significance and potential impact.

2

Verification & Authentication

Each report is verified against multiple sources to ensure we have complete, unaltered versions. We cross-check with official government publications and archival records.

3

Analysis & Summarization

Our team of researchers analyzes each report to extract key findings, recommendations, and implementation status. We create both comprehensive and simplified versions for different audiences.

4

Standardized Presentation

Reports are presented in a consistent format with uniform metadata, making comparison and reference easier. We include original documents alongside our analysis.

5

Public Engagement

We create accessible summaries, discussion guides, and educational materials to facilitate public understanding and debate around these critical documents.

Why This Archive Matters

Accountability

Provides citizens with tools to hold leaders accountable for implementing recommendations they've already paid for through taxes.

Institutional Memory

Preserves institutional knowledge that might otherwise be lost, preventing repetitive commissions on the same issues.

Research Resource

Serves as a centralized resource for researchers, journalists, students, and policymakers working on Kenyan governance issues.

Democratic Participation

Empowers citizens to participate meaningfully in governance discussions with factual knowledge of past recommendations.

Our Team & Collaborators

The Kenya Reports Archive is maintained by a dedicated group of researchers, archivists, and digital rights activists committed to open governance and public accountability. While we maintain editorial independence, we collaborate with:

Academic Institutions

Partnering with universities for research and analysis of report contents.

Civil Society

Working with NGOs to promote awareness and implementation tracking.

Media Partners

Collaborating with journalists to highlight key findings and updates.
