Commission of Inquiry

Waki Report (2008)

Released: October 2008 529 pages Commission of Inquiry into Post-Election Violence

Executive Summary

The Waki Report, officially titled "Report of the Commission of Inquiry into Post-Election Violence (CIPEV)", was established under the National Accord to investigate the 2007/08 post-election violence. Chaired by Justice Philip Waki, the commission documented systematic human rights violations and recommended accountability mechanisms.

Core Mandate:

  • Investigate facts and circumstances of post-election violence
  • Establish root causes and patterns of violence
  • Identify perpetrators and instigators
  • Recommend legal and policy reforms

1. Systematic Violence

The commission documented organized attacks with distinct patterns:

  • 1,133 confirmed deaths (405 by police gunfire)
  • Over 650,000 internally displaced persons
  • Sexual violence used as weapon (900 documented cases)
  • 117,216 private properties destroyed

2. Perpetrators & Instigators

Identified actors responsible for violence:

  • Politicians planned and financed ethnic militia groups
  • Police used excessive force (30% of all deaths)
  • Local administrators facilitated violence in 12 hotspots
  • Business leaders funded transportation of attackers

3. Institutional Failures

Systemic breakdowns that enabled violence:

  • Security forces failed to protect civilians in 8 counties
  • Judiciary lacked capacity for timely election disputes
  • Media incited violence through hate speech
  • Historical land injustices exacerbated tensions

Key Recommendations

01

Special Tribunal

Establish Kenyan-led Special Tribunal with international judges to try perpetrators

02

ICC Referral

Seek International Criminal Court intervention if Kenya fails to prosecute within 12 months

03

Police Reforms

Complete overhaul of police command structure and training

04

Truth Commission

Establish TJRC to address historical injustices fueling violence

The report famously included a secret envelope containing names of alleged perpetrators, to be handed to the ICC if Kenya failed to establish the Special Tribunal.

Implementation Status (2023 Update)

Not Implemented (40%)

Notable Developments:

  • Complete: ICC intervention (6 cases initiated though most collapsed)
  • Partial: Police reforms begun but not fully implemented
  • Partial: TJRC established but recommendations ignored
  • Not Implemented: No Special Tribunal created
