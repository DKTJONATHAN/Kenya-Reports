Judicial Transformation

Judiciary Reforms Report (2012)

Released: May 2012 182 pages Judiciary Working Committee on Reforms

Executive Summary

The Judiciary Transformation Framework (JTF) 2012-2016 was developed under Chief Justice Willy Mutunga to implement Article 159 of Kenya's Constitution. This blueprint addressed systemic failures exposed during the 2007/08 post-election crisis when courts failed to resolve electoral disputes effectively.

Historical Context:

  • 2007: High Court declined to hear election petitions citing "lack of jurisdiction"
  • 2010 Constitution created Judiciary as independent arm of government
  • 2011: Vetting of judges removed 40% of magistrates and 14 judges
  • 2012: First public interview process for judicial appointments

1. Institutional Deficiencies

The diagnostic revealed chronic weaknesses:

  • Case backlog of 1 million (some dating to 1980s)
  • Average case duration: 5.7 years for civil matters
  • Only 42% of Kenyans trusted judicial system (2011 TI survey)
  • Corruption perception index: 3.8/10 for judiciary

2. Structural Problems

Root causes of judicial inefficiency:

  • Politicized appointments process
  • Inadequate funding (0.4% of national budget)
  • Outdated case management systems (paper-based files)
  • Concentration of courts in urban areas

3. Public Trust Crisis

Key drivers of declining confidence:

  • Perceived "cash-for-rulings" culture
  • Lack of transparency in rulings
  • Deliberate delays to benefit litigants
  • Inconsistent application of laws

Key Recommendations

01

Case Clearance

Reduce case backlog by 50% through Night Courts and ADR mechanisms

02

Technology Upgrade

Implement integrated Case Tracking System (CTS) across all courts

03

Decentralization

Establish 100 new magistrate courts in underserved areas

04

Performance Metrics

Introduce individual judicial officer performance evaluations

Notable Developments:

  • Complete: CTS rolled out to 120+ courts (though with technical issues)
  • Complete: Backlog reduced to 350,000 cases by 2022
  • Partial: 78 new magistrate courts established (vs 100 target)
  • Not Implemented: Performance-based promotions not fully adopted
