Economic Blueprint

Economic Recovery Strategy (2003)

Released: June 2003 98 pages Ministry of Planning & National Development

Executive Summary

The Economic Recovery Strategy (ERS), officially titled "Economic Recovery Strategy for Wealth and Employment Creation 2003-2007", was the first major economic blueprint of the NARC government following the 2002 transition. It aimed to reverse Kenya's economic decline under the Moi administration.

Core Objectives:

  • Reverse GDP decline from 0.5% (2002) to 6-7% growth
  • Reduce fiscal deficit from 3.2% to 1.7% of GDP
  • Create 500,000 new jobs annually
  • Restore investor confidence through governance reforms

Key Findings

Fig 2: Projected sector growth under ERS (2003-2007)

1. Economic Crisis (2002)

The report documented severe economic deterioration:

  • GDP growth at 0.5% (lowest since independence)
  • Public debt at 70% of GDP
  • Unemployment at 14.6% (urban areas 22%)
  • Inflation at 9.5% (food inflation 15.2%)

2. Structural Weaknesses

Identified systemic constraints:

  • Corruption consuming 30% of national budget
  • Agricultural productivity declined by 3.2% annually
  • Infrastructure spending at 1.8% of GDP (needed 6%)
  • Private sector credit growth at 2.1% (below 15% target)

3. Governance Failures

Institutional challenges:

  • Public sector wage bill consumed 9.2% of GDP
  • Only 43% of development budget implemented
  • Judiciary case backlog of 82,000 cases
  • 50% of businesses cited corruption as top constraint

Key Strategies

01

Macroeconomic Stability

Reduce fiscal deficit from 3.2% to 1.7% through expenditure rationalization

02

Agricultural Revival

Increase sector growth from -3.2% to 5% through input subsidies and credit

03

Infrastructure Investment

Increase infrastructure spending to 6% of GDP targeting roads and energy

04

Governance Reforms

Establish anti-corruption institutions and judicial reforms

Implementation Outcomes (2007 Evaluation)

Not Achieved (30%)
Partially Achieved (45%)
Fully Achieved (25%)

Key Results:

  • Achieved: GDP growth reached 6.1% (2006)
  • Partial: Created 350,000 jobs/year (vs 500k target)
  • Partial: Reduced fiscal deficit to 2.1% (vs 1.7%)
  • Failed: Anti-corruption efforts collapsed after 2005

Sources: World Bank Kenya Economic Update (2007), KNBS Statistical Abstract (2008)
