Independent Review Commission

Kriegler Report (2008)

Released: September 2008 156 pages Independent Review Commission on General Elections

Executive Summary

The Kriegler Report, officially titled "Report of the Independent Review Commission on the General Elections Held in Kenya on 27 December 2007", was established under the National Accord following the 2007/08 post-election violence. Chaired by South African judge Johann Kriegler, the commission conducted a forensic examination of Kenya's electoral systems.

Core Mandate:

  • Investigate all aspects of 2007 general elections
  • Identify systemic weaknesses and failures
  • Determine causes of electoral violence
  • Recommend comprehensive electoral reforms

Key Findings

1. Electoral System Collapse

The commission found catastrophic failures across all electoral processes:

  • Voter register contained 1.2 million ineligible voters
  • Electronic results transmission system completely failed
  • 35% of polling stations had compromised ballot security
  • Presidential results were altered at 5 constituency tally centers

2. Institutional Failures

Structural deficiencies in electoral governance:

  • Electoral Commission of Kenya (ECK) was politically compromised
  • Lack of transparency in vote tallying process
  • Inadequate staff training (60% of officials untrained)
  • No effective mechanism for dispute resolution

3. Violence Triggers

Key factors that escalated electoral dispute into violence:

  • Politicians mobilized ethnic militia groups
  • Security forces used excessive force (405 killed by police)
  • Media fueled tensions through partisan reporting
  • Historical land grievances exploited for mobilization

Key Recommendations

01

New Electoral Body

Immediate dissolution of ECK and creation of independent professional body (led to IEBC establishment)

02

Voter Register Overhaul

Complete biometric voter registration system with regular audits

03

Legal Framework

New elections law to prevent presidential vote manipulation

04

Technology Systems

Robust electronic results transmission with paper trail

Implementation Status (2023 Update)

Not Implemented (25%)
Partial (50%)
Complete (25%)

Notable Developments:

  • Complete: IEBC established in 2011 to replace ECK
  • Partial: Biometric voter registration introduced but with challenges
  • Partial: Elections Act 2011 passed but amendments weakened reforms
  • Not Implemented: No effective punishment for electoral offenses

Sources: IEBC Annual Reports (2022), Kenya Human Rights Commission Election Audit (2022)
