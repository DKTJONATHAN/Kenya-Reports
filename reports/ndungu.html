Official Commission Report

Ndungu Land Report (2004)

Released: June 2004 342 pages Commission of Inquiry into Illegal/Irregular Allocation of Public Land

Executive Summary

The Ndungu Land Report, officially titled "Report of the Commission of Inquiry into the Illegal/Irregular Allocation of Public Land", was commissioned by President Mwai Kibaki in 2003 and chaired by lawyer Paul Ndungu. The commission investigated land grabbing and irregular allocations of public land between 1963 and 2002.

Core Mandate:

  • Identify all illegal/irregular allocations of public land
  • Determine circumstances of such allocations
  • Identify beneficiaries and public officials involved
  • Recommend legal and administrative measures

1. Scale of Illegal Allocations

The commission found over 200,000 acres of public land had been illegally allocated, including:

  • Forest reserves (48 cases)
  • Road reserves (32 cases)
  • School lands (27 cases)
  • Government houses (19 cases)

2. Modus Operandi

Common methods used in illegal allocations:

  • Backdated allotment letters
  • Forged documents
  • Bypassing proper procedures
  • Multiple allocations of same parcel

3. Notable Cases

The report highlighted specific high-profile cases:

  • Karura Forest allocations
  • Lang'ata Primary School land
  • Uhuru Park perimeter excisions
  • Mombasa Government houses

Key Recommendations

01

Land Title Revocation

Immediate revocation of all illegally acquired titles and return to public use

02

Legal Reforms

Amend land laws to prevent future illegal allocations and strengthen oversight

03

Prosecutions

Criminal prosecution of public officials and beneficiaries involved

04

Institutional Changes

Overhaul of Lands Ministry and Department of Surveys

Implementation Status (2023 Update)

Not Implemented (72%)
Partial (18%)
Complete (10%)

Notable Developments:

  • Partial: Some forest lands recovered (Karura, Ngong Forest)
  • Partial: Establishment of National Land Commission (2012)
  • Not Implemented: Majority of title revocations and prosecutions

Source: Kenya Land Alliance Monitoring Report (2021), Ministry of Lands Annual Reports
