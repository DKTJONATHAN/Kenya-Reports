Truth, Justice and Reconciliation Commission

TJRC Final Report (2013)

Released: May 2013 2,300 pages (4 volumes) Chair: Bethuel Kiplagat

Executive Summary

The TJRC Report, officially titled "Report of the Truth, Justice and Reconciliation Commission", was established under the TJRC Act of 2008 following the 2007/08 post-election violence. The commission investigated historical injustices from December 12, 1963 (independence) to February 28, 2008 (National Accord).

Core Mandate:

  • Investigate gross human rights violations since independence
  • Examine historical land injustices and illegal dispossession
  • Document political violence and ethnic clashes
  • Recommend reparations and institutional reforms

1. Systematic Human Rights Violations

The commission documented widespread atrocities:

  • Over 10,000 cases of torture by state security agencies
  • 400+ enforced disappearances (mostly during Moi era)
  • Massacres in Wagalla (1984), Mt. Elgon (2006-08), and post-election violence
  • Sexual violence as weapon during ethnic clashes

2. Land Injustices

Structural land dispossession patterns:

  • Illegal allocation of 1 million acres of public land
  • Historical injustices in Rift Valley, Coast, and Central regions
  • Sagana State House used as land grabbing center
  • Over 300,000 IDPs from land-related conflicts

3. Political Violence

Patterns of election-related violence:

  • 8 major episodes of election violence since 1969
  • State-sponsored ethnic militia in 1990s clashes
  • Politicians armed youth groups in 2007/08 violence
  • Security forces participated in ethnic killings

Key Recommendations

01

Reparations Program

Establish Sh10 billion fund for victims of historical injustices

02

Land Reforms

Revoke illegally acquired titles and create historical land injustices tribunal

03

Vetting of Officials

Remove public officers implicated in human rights violations

04

Memorialization

Build monuments and memorials at massacre sites

Implementation Status (2023 Update)

Not Implemented (70%)
Partial (25%)
Complete (5%)

Notable Developments:

  • Complete: National Coroner's Service Act passed (2017)
  • Partial: Some land cases addressed through Ndung'u Report follow-up
  • Not Implemented: No comprehensive reparations program established
  • Not Implemented: No official apology for historical injustices

Sources: KNCHR Implementation Report (2021), Kenya Land Alliance Monitoring (2022)
